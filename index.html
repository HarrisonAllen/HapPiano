HapPiano

Feel your way through music

Our Idea

It can be challenging to learn how to play the piano.

Our tactile piano gradually reduces support over time.

The idea is to detect how well the player is playing and then adjust the frequency of tactile guidance accordingly.

How it works

5 Keys

Laser cut for fast iteration

Key Mechanism

Dowel rod and spring - simple and effective

Detect Key Press

Limit Switch - Small with fast response

Tactile Nubs

Laser cut to maximize compactness and touchability

Nub Actuation

Servos for fine, inexpensive control

Control

RaspberryPi3 w/ GPIO extension - Lots of docs, Python, and more easily enables dynamic programs

Audio

Speaker connected to RaspberryPi through AUX

Software

Software adapts to user performance by increasing or decreasing frequency of tactile support.

User Study

Protocol:

  1. Users trained on tactile and normal scenarios
  2. After some practice, given new song to learn. Performance and growth analyzed
  3. Tested on new song
  4. Swap conditions and repeat

Results:

  • Good for complete beginners
  • Useless for those with some experience
  • Haptic positioning is too far back for some, and too far forwards for others. Different haptic solution would work better
  • Users used the software for feedback and analysis of performance as opposed to the tactile guidance
